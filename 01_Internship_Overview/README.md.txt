# Project Overview

This repository documents my Summer Research Internship carried out at the Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati.

The objective of the internship was to study the complete engineering workflow involved in developing a digitally controlled synchronous buck converter using Model-Based Design techniques and deploying the controller on a Texas Instruments C2000 Digital Signal Processor.

The project combines theoretical concepts with practical implementation, beginning with system modelling in MATLAB and Simulink, progressing through digital controller design, and culminating in embedded firmware development, hardware validation, and laboratory testing.

---

# Internship Objectives

The primary objectives of the internship were:

- Learn Model-Based Design using MATLAB and Simulink.
- Develop a high-fidelity digital twin of a synchronous buck converter.
- Design a cascaded digital controller.
- Study digital control techniques for power converters.
- Learn the Texas Instruments C2000 development ecosystem.
- Configure and program embedded peripherals.
- Validate controller performance using laboratory equipment.
- Document the complete engineering workflow.
